Revo Zero
[COMPLETE]
Duration: 1 month Team Size: 3 developers Budget: Sleepless Nights
Revo Zero was made in under a month by 3 developers, and is a game where you play as a young kid who is trying to make their way through the "Revo Zero tornument", The game has a unique and clean physics system, which allows the tops on the screen to affect each other significantly and still control great. I made multiple changes to the physics in order to enhance fps from 15 to 90. There's also unique mechanics involving "tracing" a circle around the enemy. It's a quick but difficult play that involves a lot of skill.

Technologies

Godot 2D physics simulation Object Oriented Programming

CRITTER CRUSHERS
[COMPLETE]
Duration: 2 days Team Size: 1 developer Budget: Hope
Critter Crusher was a tower defense game which had you growing plants in order to defend the roots of a tree from bugs. It was awarded runner-up at the Georgia Tech Global Game Jam and made in 48 hours. It was developed bottom up, that had easily expandable features, with multiple different types of towers, tower upgrades and enemies. The game has a map that changes over time as the roots of the tree grow.

Technologies

Unity Pathfinding C#

Campus Cats App
[COMPLETED]
Duration: 6 months Team Size: 4 engineers Budget: $50
Campus Cats is a mobile app for Georgia Tech’s Campus Cats club, enabling users to report cat sightings, upload photos, chat with members, and access information about campus cats. The app aims to foster communication and engagement within the Campus Cats community while helping track and care for stray cats on campus. Creating the app involved extensive work with Firebase for real-time data synchronization, TypeScript for the frontend, and Node.js for the backend.

Manga OCR
[COMPLETE]
Duration: 4 months Team Size: 3 researchers
Developed an OCR (Optical Character Recognition) system specifically for manga, enabling text extraction from scanned manga pages. We take the manga text bubbles, segment the characters, and turn every character into machine-readable text. Using this, we can translate entire sentences into english with just the text bubble as input. We had a ResNet model, a tesseract model, and a Vision Encoder Decoder Transformer model.

Technologies

OCR Computer Vision Machine Learning Python
